BOM Generation
Active
What It Does

Merges multiple individual panel Excel sheets into a single unified Bill of Materials. Cleans and deduplicates part numbers, sums quantities across panels, and matches against MRS data.

Requirements

Each panel sheet must be an Excel file (.xls / .xlsx). All files must share the same column structure — Part Number, Make, Description, and Qty.

.xls.xlsxSame column layout
Excel Format Expected

Col A: Sr No · Col B: Make · Col C: Part Number · Col D: Description · Col E: Qty. Data starts from row 2.

Col B → MakeCol C → Part NoCol E → Qty
What You Get

A single merged Excel with all parts consolidated. Duplicate part numbers are summed. Output is ready to use as your project BOM.

Merged BOMDeduped partsSummed qty

MRS Generation
New
What It Does

Takes your EPLAN export files and a blank MRS Master template, extracts all part data, and writes it directly into the MRS format — producing a filled, ready-to-use MRS file.

Requirements

One or more EPLAN files (.xls / .xlsx) as data source. One blank MRS Master template to fill. Additional field values entered manually in Step 2.

1+ EPLAN files1 Blank Master
Excel Format Expected

EPLAN files: configurable column mapping (Part No, Description, Make, Qty). MRS Master: your standard blank template — format and headers preserved as-is.

EPLAN col mappingMRS col mapping
What You Get

A fully filled MRS file built on your master template — all EPLAN parts mapped in, section info filled, ready to drop straight into the MRS Merger tool.

Filled MRSMaster format keptMRS Merger ready

MRS Merger
Active
What It Does

Takes a blank Master MRS template and merges multiple MRS files into it. For each file, Sheet 1 is pasted first, then Sheet 2 right after — sequentially, one after another.

Requirements

One Master Sheet (blank template with formatting). One or more MRS files to merge. Each MRS file must have data on its second sheet.

1 Master file1+ Merge filesData on Sheet 2
Excel Format Expected

Master: formatted blank template, rows 1–7 are headers. Merge files: Sheet 1 and Sheet 2 both contain MRS data rows to be carried over as-is.

Master = templateSheet 1 + Sheet 2Rows 1–7 = headers
What You Get

A single merged MRS Excel built on the master template. All data from each file's Sheet 1 and Sheet 2 is appended in order, preserving master formatting.

Merged MRSMaster formattingSequential paste

Procurement Planner
Beta
What It Does

Compares your BOM (what's needed per panel) against your MRS (what's been ordered per section) and tells you exactly which parts are in excess, shortage, or exact — broken down by section.

Requirements

One BOM sheet with panel columns from col E onwards. One MRS sheet with Part Number in col C, Section in col G, Qty in col P.

1 BOM file1 MRS filePanel headers row 1
Excel Format Expected

BOM: Row 1 = panel headers (e.g. ZA_P1_PANE_1), Row 2 onwards = part data. MRS: Col C = Part No, Col G = Section, Col P = Qty.

BOM row 1 = headersMRS col G = sectionMRS col P = qty
What You Get

A visual breakdown per section showing panel quantities vs MRS ordered. Export a structured Excel with per-section columns, MRS qty, and colour-coded excess/shortage/exact status.

Excess flaggedShortage flaggedSummary col

Stock Check
New
What It Does

Cross-references your BOM part numbers against your Open Stock file. Instantly shows which parts are already available in the warehouse so you know what doesn't need to be ordered.

Requirements

One BOM file (.xls / .xlsx) with part numbers in a configurable column. One Open Stock file in the standard format — Part No in col F, Qty in col H, Status in col D.

1 BOM file1 Open Stock fileCol D = OPEN filter
Stock File Format

Standard Open Stock format: Col D = Office status (only "OPEN" rows counted), Col F = Type No (Part Number), Col H = In Stock Qty. Data starts from row 3.

Col F → Part NoCol H → QtyCol D → OPEN
What You Get

A 2-sheet Excel export. Sheet 1 is your full BOM with matching parts highlighted. Sheet 2 is the Open Stock — filtered to only rows whose part number appears in your BOM.

BOM highlightedFiltered stock sheetIn-stock qty column
